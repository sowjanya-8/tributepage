Luis von Ahn

The man who created a free language learning app.

Here's a time line of Luis van Ahn

  • 1978: Luis von Ahn is born on August 19 in Guatemala City, Guatemala.
  • 2000:Von Ahn earns his bachelor's degree in mathematics from Duke University.
  • 2003:He completes his Ph.D. in computer science at Carnegie Mellon University. His doctoral dissertation, titled "Labeling Images with a Computer Game," laid the foundation for the concept of human computation.
  • 2005:Luis von Ahn co-founds reCAPTCHA, a technology that combines human computation and computer security. It uses human input to digitize books and improve computer vision algorithms.
  • 2009:Google acquires reCAPTCHA, and von Ahn becomes a professor at Carnegie Mellon University.
  • 2011:Von Ahn co-founds Duolingo, a language-learning platform that offers free language education courses. Duolingo uses a gamified approach to engage users in language learning.
  • 2012:Duolingo is officially launched to the public.
  • 2014:Duolingo introduces a language certification program, allowing users to take language proficiency tests.
  • 2017: Luis von Ahn is named one of the "Top 10 Most Innovative Companies in Education" by Fast Company.
  • 2019:Duolingo reaches 300 million users worldwide.
  • 2021:Duolingo goes public with an initial public offering (IPO) on the NASDAQ stock exchange under the ticker symbol "DUOL."
